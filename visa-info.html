Do I Need a Visa?

Your guide to entering Sri Lanka hassle-free

Contents
Overview The eVisa Process Entry Requirements Visa on Arrival Exemptions

1. Overview

For most international travelers, the answer is yes. Visitors to Sri Lanka for tourism, business, or transit purposes require an Electronic Travel Authorization (ETA) or eVisa prior to their arrival. The process has been highly digitized, making it straightforward to apply online before you travel.

2. The eVisa Process

The official portal for obtaining a Sri Lankan visa is the eVisa website. We strongly recommend applying at least a few days before your flight, although approvals are often processed within 24 hours.

  • Application: You can apply online by filling out your passport details and travel itinerary.
  • Fee: A processing fee applies depending on your nationality (SAARC vs. non-SAARC countries).
  • Approval: You will receive a confirmation notice via email. It is advisable to carry a printed copy of this approval along with your passport.

3. Entry Requirements

Alongside your approved eVisa, immigration officials at the airport will check that you meet standard entry requirements:

  • A passport valid for not less than 6 months from the date of arrival.
  • A confirmed return ticket or onward journey ticket.
  • Sufficient funds to meet the expenses during your stay.

4. Visa on Arrival

While an on-arrival visa counter exists at the Bandaranaike International Airport (BIA) in Colombo, we highly advise securing your eVisa in advance to avoid long queues and potential administrative delays after a long flight.

5. Exemptions

Citizens of Singapore, Maldives, and Seychelles are currently exempt from the ETA requirement and can obtain a visa on arrival free of charge. Please verify with your local embassy as these policies can occasionally change.
