Surf Paradise

Surfing in Arugam Bay

4.9 (200+ reviews) | Eastern Province, Sri Lanka

Arugam Bay, perched along the sun-drenched eastern coast of Sri Lanka, is widely regarded as one of the top 10 surf destinations in the world. This crescent-shaped bay has drawn wave riders from every continent since the 1970s, offering a rare combination of world-class point breaks, warm tropical waters, and a laid-back beach culture that makes every surfer feel at home.

The main break at Arugam Bay — simply known as "The Point" — is a long, peeling right-hand point break that rolls over a sandy reef bottom for up to 300 metres on a good day. When the south-east monsoon swells arrive between April and October, the bay transforms into a playground of perfectly groomed waves, attracting professionals and amateurs alike. The wave is famously forgiving: powerful enough to challenge advanced surfers, yet mellow enough for intermediates to lock into long, memorable rides.

Beyond the main break, the surrounding coastline is dotted with a series of lesser-known surf spots, each with its own personality. From the hollow barrels of Whiskey Point to the beginner-friendly whitewater of Peanut Farm, and the remote perfection of Okanda, there is a wave here for every skill level. When the ocean finally calls it a day, Arugam Bay's sandy lanes come alive with beachside cafés, local seafood grills, and the kind of sunsets that make you forget the rest of the world entirely.

Signature Surf Experiences

The Main Point

The crown jewel of Arugam Bay — a long, peeling right-hand point break that offers rides up to 300 metres. Best between May and September when the monsoon swells deliver clean, consistent waves with offshore winds.

Whiskey Point

Located just north of the bay, Whiskey Point serves up fast, hollow barrels that attract experienced surfers. The beach break offers both left and right-hand waves, with a beautiful stretch of empty sand to relax on between sessions.

Surf Schools & Lessons

Never surfed before? No problem. Multiple surf schools along the bay offer board rentals, beginner lessons, and video coaching. Peanut Farm and the inside section of The Point are ideal for first-timers to catch their first waves.

Beach Culture & Vibes

After sunset, the sandy lanes of Arugam Bay transform into a vibrant social scene. Enjoy fresh seafood at beachside grills, sip king coconut at surf shacks, and mingle with a global community of travelers drawn by the ocean's magnetic pull.

Traveler's Guide

Best Time to Surf

April to October brings the south-east monsoon swell. Peak season is June to August with the most consistent, clean waves.

Getting There

Approximately 7 hours from Colombo by road. The nearest domestic airport is in Batticaloa (1.5 hours away). Tuk-tuks and motorbikes are the best local transport.

Suggested Duration

5 to 7 days to surf multiple breaks, explore the nearby lagoons and Kumana National Park, and truly absorb the east coast lifestyle.

Surf Tip

Arrive at The Point before 6:30 AM to score uncrowded waves. Bring reef booties for the rocky entry, and apply reef-safe sunscreen to protect both yourself and the marine ecosystem.
