Kandy

4.7 (160+ reviews) | Central Highlands, Sri Lanka

Nestled in the heart of the mist-covered Central Highlands, Kandy stands as the last royal capital of ancient Sri Lanka. It is a sacred sanctuary of culture, spiritual devotion, and lush highland heritage, where the ancient Temple of the Sacred Tooth Relic overlooks the tranquil waters of Kandy Lake.

For centuries, Kandy served as the administrative stronghold of the Kandyan Kings, successfully resisting colonial forces long after the coast had fallen. Today, this UNESCO World Heritage city serves as the spiritual and cultural capital of the island. Surrounded by steep, forested mountains, the city has a unique, peaceful atmosphere. Its central lake, built in 1807 by the last king, offers serene pathways shaded by old trees, while local streets hum with the sounds of traditional artisans and bustling spice markets.

Whether you are participating in the evening prayers at the golden-roofed Temple of the Tooth, walking among the giant palms and rare orchids of the Royal Botanical Gardens, or witnessing the energetic drumming and fire-walking of Kandyan dancers, Kandy is a deep dive into the soul of Sri Lanka. It is a destination where old world traditions continue to shape the flow of modern life.

Signature Experiences in Kandy

Temple of the Tooth

Sri Lanka's most sacred Buddhist temple, housing the relic of the tooth of the Buddha. Admire the golden canopy, intricate wood carvings, and join the worshippers who gather in white during the prayer ceremonies.

Peradeniya Botanical Gardens

Spanning 147 acres along the Mahaweli River, these historic gardens are renowned for their avenue of giant palm trees, rare orchids, medicinal plants, and a massive centenary Java fig tree covering the lawn.

Kandy Lake & Viewpoint

Take a slow walk around Kandy Lake, passing the decorative stone wall. Later, travel up the hillside to the city viewpoint to take in the panoramic view of the lake, temple, and town below.

Kandyan Dance & Drums

Witness an energetic performance of traditional Kandyan dance. The show features dancers in silver costumes performing athletic spins, leaps, rhythmic drumming, and concludes with a fire-walking demonstration.

Traveler's Guide

Best Time to Visit

January to April and July/August (during the Esala Perahera parade, one of Asia's most spectacular religious festivals).

Getting There

Take the train from Colombo for a relaxing climb into the mountains (3 hours), or hire a private vehicle via the highway and hill roads.

Suggested Duration

1 to 2 days to explore the main temples, walk the gardens, and experience a evening dance performance.

Serene Tip

Visit the Temple of the Tooth during the evening puja ceremony (around 6:30 PM). The sound of traditional Kandyan oboes and drums makes it a spiritual experience.
